my story & an explanation.

For the longest time, I was certain that my passion was in research.

As you might have noticed (or experienced for yourself), passions change.

A quick glance at my resume might suggest that my interests are also bound by the laws of entropy.
However, I promise there is a method to my madness.

Chemistry is magic. By leveraging our understanding of the world, we can direct atoms from different molecules to come together, making something entirely new. Named reactions are like tried and true spells. And with each attempt to synthesize a new drug, we get closer to changing the lives of patients around the world. I earned my B.S. in Nanoengineering and Chemistry at UCSD and worked in the research & development of treatments for: cancer, cystic fibrosis, and pain.

With each company I worked at and each project I worked on, my world expanded. A project I led became a catalyst for change.
I developed soft skills as a project manager and worked with cross-functional teams across chemistry, biology, hardware engineering and software engineering to build an automated tool to accelerate the drug discovery process. In an effort to better communicate user requirements with our engineers and analyze the large, multidimensional datasets we were generating, I began to teach myself Python and R. Realizing that I wanted to establish fundamentals in programming, I am now pursuing a B.S. in Computer Science at Oregon State University.
