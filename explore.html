the thing about passion is ...

You peek down some roads and took a few steps before turning back.

You followed some roads that led to a dead end, and others that got lost at too many intersections.

Ultimately, all roads are connected to all other roads.

There is certainly a beauty to sticking with something and seeing it through until the very end.
On the other hand, I am constantly reminded of the "sunken cost fallacy". So, how do we navigate these conflicting feelings?

I read and write as a way to better understand both myself, and the world around me. One quote that has stuck with me goes as follows:

There is no right or wrong, only a series of possibilities that shift with each thought, feeling, and action that you experience.
There exists a tension between acting on one's best knowledge, and waiting in an effort to gather more.
I have my own set of rules and thresholds to guide the decisions I make: explore/exploit, optimal stopping, etc.
I believe what's most important in this process is the capacity to evaluate different circumstances and consider second and third order effects.
