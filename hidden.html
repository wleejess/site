my story & an explanation.
a more detailed about me

For the longest time, I was certain that my passion was in medicine.

As you might have noticed (or experienced for yourself), passions change.

A quick glance at my resume might suggest that I am simply following the laws of entropy.
However, I promise there is a method to my madness.

Science is a form of magic. Growing up, I wanted nothing more than to leverage my understanding of the world and collaborate with others to make new innovations. I earned my B.S. in Nanoengineering and Chemistry at UCSD and worked in the research & development of: cancer, cystic fibrosis, and chronic pain.

Throughout my time as both a student and researcher, I further refined my strengths and interests. I developed soft skills as a project manager, and learned to analyze large, multidimensional datasets using Python. While I am grateful for my experiences in life sciences, I realized that it was time for a change. My next steps from there were to get my CAPM (Certified Associate in Project Management) certification, and to establish fundamentals in programming by pursuing a second B.S. in Computer Science at Oregon State University.

I will use these combined experiences to help develop tools that researchers can use to streamline their work: everything from documenting experiments in detail to analyzing complex datasets. As a former scientist, I can understand and empathize with their needs. Moreover, as I'm learning more about computer science (data structures, algorithms, web development, databases), I am confident in my abilities to translate user needs into detailed requirements to guide the development and implementation of new programs.
